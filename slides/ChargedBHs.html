Review of Charged Black Holes

GR admits very simple charged solutions:

\[ I_{0}[g_{\mu\nu},A_{\mu}]=-\frac{1}{16\pi}\int\rd^{4}x\sqrt{-g}\, \left(R-F_{\mu\nu}^2\right) \quad \quad \left(G_N=1\right) \]
\[ \begin{gather} g_{\mu\nu}\rd x^{\mu}\rd x^{\nu}=-\Delta \rd t^{2}+\Delta^{-1}\rd r^{2}+r^{2}\rd \Omega^{2}\ , \quad A_{\mu}\rd x^{\mu}=\frac{Q}{r}\rd t\nn \Delta=\left(1-\frac{2M}{r}+\frac{Q^{2}}{r^{2}}\right) \end{gather} \]

\(M,Q\) determine horizon. Must have \(M>Q\), else Naked Singularity

Review of Charged BH Thermodynamics

Famously, black holes behave thermodynamically

Temperature and Entropy given by

\[ \begin{gather} T_0(M,Q)=\frac{r_{+}-M}{2\pi r_{+}^{2}}\quad \quad S_{0}(M,Q)=\frac{\rm Area}{4}=\pi r_{+}^{2}\nn r_{+}\equiv M+\sqrt{M^{2}-Q^{2}} \end{gather} \]

Must again have \(M>Q\), otherwise \(T,S\) are imaginary

Extremal Mass

\[ M(Q,T)> \lim_{T\to 0}M(Q,T)=Q\equiv M_{\rm ext}^{0}(Q) \]
